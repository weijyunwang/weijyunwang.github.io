Catalysis & Process Systems for a Sustainable Future

The CPE Lab develops high-performance electrocatalysts and thermocatalysts for green energy conversion, and translates laboratory findings into industrial-scale process designs through rigorous simulation, techno-economic analysis, and life-cycle assessment.

Research

Our research follows a clear step-by-step progression: from developing catalysts for target energy conversion reactions, to formulating kinetic rate equations from experimental data, to designing industrial-scale processes, and finally to evaluating economic feasibility and environmental impact.

1. Catalyst Development for Energy Conversion

We develop catalysts for three key energy conversion reactions using two principal material platforms: metal-organic framework (MOF)-based catalysts, which offer high surface area, tunable porosity, and strong metal-support interactions; and high-entropy alloy (HEA) catalysts, which leverage multi-element synergy including high-entropy, cocktail, sluggish diffusion, and lattice distortion effects to simultaneously enhance activity, selectivity, and long-term stability. In situ spectroscopic techniques, including liquid-phase TEM (ILTEM) and synchrotron X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS), are employed under working conditions to elucidate deactivation mechanisms and establish structure-activity relationships.

Thermal Catalysis

Methanol Synthesis

CO₂ hydrogenation to methanol over copper-based catalysts. Research focuses on enhancing catalyst hydrophobicity to suppress water-induced sintering and active site blockage, improving long-term stability under high-pressure operating conditions.

Electrocatalysis

Oxygen Reduction Reaction

HEA electrocatalysts as cost-effective alternatives to Pt for the oxygen reduction reaction (ORR) in fuel cells. Multi-element alloying tunes the d-band center and optimizes oxygen intermediate adsorption strength for enhanced activity and durability.

Electrocatalysis

Methanol Electrooxidation

MOF-based electrocatalysts for methanol electrooxidation in alkaline electrolyte, targeting H₂ recovery via electrochemical reforming. Electronic modulation through metal-MOF interactions suppresses CO poisoning and enhances catalytic performance.

2. Reactor Modeling & Design

We design and construct both thermocatalytic fixed-bed reactors and continuous-flow electrochemical reactors at the bench scale. Kinetic models are integrated with reactor design equations to optimize conversion, selectivity, and energy efficiency. For electrochemical reactors, membrane-free two-electrode configurations enclosed between stainless-steel endplates and Teflon flow channels allow operation at pressures up to 80 bar. Key performance metrics are evaluated systematically to provide the experimental foundation for scale-up and process design.

3. Reaction Rate Equation Formulation

Kinetic data collected from laboratory-scale reactors are analyzed through nonlinear regression to formulate intrinsic rate expressions. For thermocatalytic reactions, Langmuir-Hinshelwood and power-law models capture surface reaction mechanisms. For electrochemical reactions, power-law rate expressions correlating reaction rates with reactant concentration and applied potential are developed. These validated kinetic models are implemented in MATLAB and integrated into Aspen Plus as user-defined reactor blocks, forming the quantitative foundation for downstream process design.

4. System-Level Process Design

Using Aspen Plus, we perform rigorous steady-state and dynamic simulations of full energy supply chains, encompassing carrier synthesis (methanol, ammonia), purification, cracking and reforming, heat integration, and power generation. Process intensification strategies and sensitivity analyses are applied to minimize energy consumption and identify optimal operating conditions.

5. Techno-Economic Analysis & Life Cycle Assessment

Building on the process simulation results, we perform comprehensive techno-economic analysis and life cycle assessment to bridge the gap between laboratory discovery and commercial deployment. The techno-economic analysis quantifies capital expenditure, operating expenditure, net present value, internal rate of return, and levelized cost of electricity under multiple sensitivity scenarios, informing investment decisions for industry partners. The life cycle assessment evaluates greenhouse gas emissions and cumulative energy demand across the full supply chain from raw material production to end use, providing the environmental performance data needed to guide sustainable process development and support national energy policy.
